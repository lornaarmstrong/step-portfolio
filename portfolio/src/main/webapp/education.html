Educational History

This page gives a brief overview of my educational history, from the ages of 11 to 20! The map shows the different educational institutions that I have attended, or currently attend. As you can see, I went to school and college in England, and I now study in Scotland for my university degree.

Map

Staindrop Academy

SEP 2011 - JUNE 2016

I attended Staindrop Academy (also called Staindrop School - An Academy), and studied 13 GCSEs, in the following subjects: Maths, English Lit, English Lang, Biology, Chemistry, Phyics, Religious Education, Graphic Products, Geography, French, German, Computer Science, and ICT. In terms of extra-curricular activities, I always involved in sport (excluding trampolining, which certainly wasn't my favourite!), from boxing to ballet, football to cross country. I was Sports Captain for my school house, Deputy Head Girl, Prefect and silver DofE award holder.

Queen Elizabeth Sixth Form College

SEP 2016 - JUNE 2018

To study for my A Levels, I attended QE Sixth Form College in Darlington. It is a high achieving college, and has almost 2000 students across both years. The college gets its name from Queen Elizabeth I as she comissioned a grammar school which was the old occupier of the site. I did A Levels in: French, Maths, Further Maths and Computer Science. In regards to extra-curricular activites, I was a member of Russian and Latin lunch clubs, I was a Student Ambassador, and part of a team of 4 students working on French Mentoring program.

University of Edinburgh

SEP 2018 - CURRENT

I am currently studying as a Computer Science undergraduate, at the University of Edinburgh. The 4-year degree covers a range of subjects and modules, but my favourites so far have been: Introduction to Data and Analysis, and Introduction to Software Engineering. As my elective courses, outside of my degree area, I chose to study Spanish and Arabic. I am also a member of Comp Soc, WomenInSTEM and Swing Dance societies. In addition, I spend one hour a week volunteering with a local Rainbow GirlGuide group.

What's Next?

I have no idea! I'd love to study or work abroad, to practice using my language skills, to see a little bit more of the world, and to meet as many new people as I can. Until then, the focus is on making the most of my two years left at university, before graduating in the summer of 2022.
